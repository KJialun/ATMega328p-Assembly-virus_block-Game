When the input rises, the output will suddenly peak. Explain the mechanism of this peaking (hint: exercise session 1).

Your answer:

because at T0, there is no current flow in Cgd,  So,  the voltage of Cgd (capacitor between gate and drain)has to remain constant (Vg=Vd).  But when input Vg suddenly increases at T0,  in order to keep Vg=Vd,  Vd will also suddenly increase at T0,  then, because r0 exist at drain side, according to Ohm's Law,  at T0 will generate a huge current, this huge current will suddenly discharge Cgd,  this is what causes the output suddenly peak.
